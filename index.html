About me

A dedicated researcher, free spirit, and highly determined individual, driven by passion, discipline, and continuous self-improvement.

PhD student and researcher in Health Sciences at the Higher Institute of Health Sciences, Hassan First University of Settat, specializing in healthcare simulation, simulation-based pedagogy, intercultural competence, and stress management in simulated environments.

My work focuses on the design and optimization of advanced simulation-based learning systems, the objective assessment of learner performance, and the development of data-informed educational strategies that enhance clinical, communication, and intercultural competencies. It integrates simulation science, educational theory, and applied behavioral approaches to improve stress regulation, reflective practice, and professional adaptability in complex healthcare settings.


What I do

  • Scientific Research

    As a PhD candidate at Hassan First University, I focus on simulation-based methodologies in health education. My work bridges healthcare simulation, pedagogy, and intercultural competence to enhance learning outcomes, stress management, and evidence-based training in complex clinical environments.

  • Scientific research in healthcare simulation

    Conducting research aimed at analyzing and improving the effectiveness of healthcare simulation through literature review and the evaluation of evidence-based educational practices.

  • Development of simulation-based educational programs

    Designing and developing simulation training programs, including defining learning objectives, targeted competencies, and appropriate simulation modalities.

  • Learners’ psychological safety

    Studying and promoting a safe learning environment that fosters confidence, active participation, and stress management during simulation activities.

  • Simulation-based teaching

    Facilitating simulation sessions, supervising learners, and conducting structured debriefings to enhance reflective learning and clinical skills development.

  • Simulation scenario design

    Designing realistic clinical scenarios with defined learning objectives, adapted to learners’ levels and targeted competencies.

  • University teaching

    Delivering undergraduate courses in biology, clinical skills, and pharmacology, including lectures, practical sessions (laboratory work/TP), and tutorial sessions (TD), contributing to the academic training of health sciences students.
